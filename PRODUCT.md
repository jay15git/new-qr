## Design Context

### Users
This product is for normal users creating QR codes and shaping them into usable visual assets. They are not operating a dense enterprise console. They need a creation surface that feels precise, calm, and trustworthy while they move between starting points, tools, and styling controls.

### Brand Personality
Mechanical, restrained, confident.

The interface should feel like a drafting surface rather than a glossy app: monochrome, sparse, exact, and tactile through linework, shadow mass, and spacing discipline instead of decorative color. It should feel intentional and tool-like without becoming cold or terminal-styled.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode is the default and current source of truth. The visual direction is industrial drafting tool: monochrome field, charcoal ink, dashed structural lines, plus-corner markers, and dense black selected states. The memorable quality should be the contrast between almost invisible default controls and sharply planted active controls.

The system should explicitly avoid soft cards, rounded pills, color accents, glow, and generic dashboard chrome.

### Design Principles
1. Make structure visible through linework and alignment, not decoration.
2. Keep inactive controls nearly printed into the surface; let active controls condense into dense monochrome mass.
3. Use shadow as physical depth, not atmosphere.
4. Prefer sharp geometry and measured spacing over friendly softness.
5. Build primitives that can scale from navigation probes to QR styling controls without changing visual language.
